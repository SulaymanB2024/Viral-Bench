# Gemini / Veo Prompt — Internships.com Proof Gap

Generate an unapproved draft for a vertical short-form career video.

## Output

- Portrait 9:16.
- 16 seconds total.
- 720p or better.
- Natural phone-camera energy with polished kinetic compositing.
- One continuous original scene with coherent character, desk, lighting, and
  object continuity.
- No baked-in captions, logos, watermarks, or legible product UI. Text overlays
  will be added locally after review.

## Scene

A fictional college student sits at a small apartment desk at dusk. The style is
warm, contemporary, slightly playful, and recognizably student-budget rather
than corporate. Use deep ink, warm cream, orange, and restrained purple accents.
Do not resemble a real creator or public figure.

From 0–3 seconds, the student rapidly opens several generic internship
application cards on a laptop. The cards pile up and become visually noisy. A
single generic role-requirement card slides toward a blank resume card and falls
through a clean animated gap between them.

From 3–9 seconds, the student pauses, closes the extra application cards, and
places three truthful evidence cards into the gap: a class project, a student
club responsibility, and a personal build. Each card is represented by a simple
icon and physical object, not legible text.

From 9–13 seconds, the three evidence cards lock into a neat bridge. One focused
application card travels safely across the bridge into a review tray. The
student reads it, corrects one detail, and visibly approves the final version.

From 13–16 seconds, end on the student in control with the tidy proof bridge,
one focused application, and a clear empty lower-third area for a later
Internships.com CTA.

## Native audio

Use subtle desk sounds, soft interface clicks, and an understated optimistic
beat. If spoken audio is generated, use this exact neutral voiceover:

“No experience? Don’t invent it. Close the proof gap with coursework, projects,
or club work. Then tailor, review, and apply.”

## Safety and originality

- Do not show Handshake, LinkedIn, TikTok, a real employer, or any competitor
  logo or recognizable UI.
- Do not reproduce any researched creator’s face, voice, wording, footage,
  setting, or shot order.
- Do not imply automatic submission.
- Do not promise an internship, interview, response, referral, or offer.
- Do not invent an employer, school, credential, or student achievement.
- Keep every application and resume element generic and unreadable.
- Return a draft for human review only; it is not approved for posting.
