# Internships.com Category Content Feed Notes

Owned product source: `https://www.internships.com/`

Social source state: no official short-video account has been confirmed from an
owned Internships.com source. The feed therefore uses a clearly labeled
category proxy:

- `internship search tips`;
- `internship application mistakes`;
- `internship openings`.

Returned creators remain attributed to themselves. They are not described as
Internships.com creators.

## Bounded sample

- 36 unique category posts;
- 18 recent-cohort posts;
- 18 popular-cohort posts;
- no downloaded media.

The observed median view count was `18,227`. This is a query sample across many
creators and must not be interpreted as an account benchmark.

## Category grammar

The strongest recurring families were:

1. **Opportunity alert**
   - employer or program;
   - internship year or season;
   - paid or remote marker;
   - role families;
   - deadline;
   - application location;
   - follow CTA for future alerts.
2. **Mistake prevention**
   - “avoid these mistakes”;
   - same résumé everywhere;
   - applying late;
   - skipping events, referrals, or role-specific proof.
3. **Application breakdown**
   - steps;
   - requirements;
   - skills;
   - benefits;
   - how and when to apply.
4. **Outcome-led proof**
   - how a creator got an internship;
   - interview or offer counts;
   - resources or sites used.

Recurring language included `internship`, `summer`, `application`, `apply`,

## Internships.com adaptation

Internships.com should combine the category's urgency and specificity with a
more trustworthy proof system:

- **Opportunity radar** — source, role, date verified, fit notes, and one
  explicit uncertainty label.
- **Application leak check** — one avoidable mismatch and one truthful fix.
- **Proof gap** — turn coursework, a project, or a club responsibility into one
  accurate requirement match.
- **People path** — one research question for an alumnus, never a promised
  referral.
- **Student review** — show that the student checks and sends the final
  application.

This creates a distinct style: current utility, clear evidence, direct language,
and visible student control.

## Boundaries

- Recheck every opening and deadline with a first-party source before
  publication.
- Do not repeat interview counts, offer counts, referral multipliers, or
  guaranteed outcomes without owned evidence.
- Do not imply affiliation with a creator or employer.
- Do not turn the category proxy into an official Internships.com feed.
