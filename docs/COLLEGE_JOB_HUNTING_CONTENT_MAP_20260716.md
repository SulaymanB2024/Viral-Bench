# College Job-Hunting and Internship Content Map — 2026-07-16

## Scope

This map combines:

- three locally stored, Apify-derived TikTok datasets;
- current web research from university career centers, Handshake, NACE, the
  FTC, news sources, podcasts, and student communities;
- content patterns rather than reusable competitor scripts or media.

It covers college students, recent graduates, international students,
first-generation students, students without formal experience, and students
trying to turn an internship into a full-time offer.

## What students are dealing with now

The strongest current context is:

1. **More competition for fewer internship listings.** Handshake reported that
   internship postings fell by more than 15% from January 2023 to January 2025
   while student applications increased.
2. **Skills and experience matter more than GPA alone.** NACE reported that
   fewer than 40% of surveyed employers screened by GPA, while nearly two-thirds
   used skills-based hiring all or most of the time.
3. **Applying at scale is emotionally punishing.** Current student discussions
   repeatedly describe hundreds of applications, ghosting, rejection fatigue,
   comparison with peers, and uncertainty about whether the problem is the
   résumé, experience, timing, or market.
4. **AI makes applying easier and standing out harder.** Career centers and
   hiring experts recommend using AI for critique, research, tailoring, and
   practice, while keeping the evidence and final voice human.
5. **Networking remains useful but poorly explained.** Students hear “network”
   constantly but often do not know whom to contact, what to ask, how to follow
   up, or how to avoid sounding transactional.
6. **Access is uneven.** International, first-generation, low-income, rural,
   transfer, community-college, and nontraditional students face additional
   constraints involving work authorization, unpaid work, geography, time,
   transportation, and smaller inherited networks.
7. **Scams target urgency and inexperience.** Fake recruiters, fake checks,
   paid “training,” personal-assistant roles, and pay-to-intern schemes remain
   important student-safety topics.

## Evidence and originality boundaries

- Public performance metadata is observational, not causal.
- Search-query results remain attributed to their creators.
- Do not copy titles, scripts, footage, visual identity, music, or likeness.
- Recheck every opportunity, deadline, eligibility rule, and compensation claim
  against a first-party source.
- Do not promise an internship, referral, interview, response, return offer, or
  full-time offer.
- Do not invent experience or use AI to fabricate evidence.
- Keep the student responsible for reviewing and submitting final application
  materials.
